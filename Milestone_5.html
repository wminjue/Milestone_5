Anime: Rise of a Global Phenomenon

First created in 1917 with propaganda films such as “Momotarō: Umi no Shinpei”, Japanese animation (anime) swept the world in the 1980s as a global entertainment phenonemon with the rise of mecha, superhero series, and space operas. As the focus of anime shifted from film studio features such as Akira and Spirited Away, the format of popular anime transformed into increasingly long series consisting of shorter episodes —— bite sized-chunks which can be pitched as more accessible, lower commitment, and reduced-stakes trial runs to mold to ever-shifting popular opinion.
